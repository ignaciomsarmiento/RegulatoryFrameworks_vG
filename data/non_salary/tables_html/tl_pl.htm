Country Paid leave Legislation
Argentina Varying by length of tenure:
- Under 5 years: 14 calendar days.
- At least 5 years and under 10: 21 calendar days.
- At least 10 years and under 20: 28 calendar days.
- At least 20 years: 35 calendar days.		 Law 20744 (1973).  
Bolivia Varying by length of tenure:
- At least 1 year: 15 working days.
- At least 5 years: 20 working days.
- At least 10 years: 30 working days.		 General Labor Law (1942, last reformed in 2017).
Brazil Varying by annual unjustified leave days:
- Less than 5 absences: 30 calendar days.
- 6 to 14 absences: 24 calendar days.
- 15 to 23 absences: 18 calendar days.
- 24 to 32 absences: 12 calendar days.		 Decree Law 5.452 (1943).
Chile At least 1 year: 15 working days. Law 18620 (1987).
Colombia 15 working days. Law Decree 2663 (1950).
Dominican Republic Varying by length of tenure:
- At least 1 year and under 5: 14 working days.
- At least 5 years: 18 working days.		 Law 97 (1997).
Ecuador At least 1 year: 15 calendar days.

After 5 years, workers have the right to one additional vacation day per additional year worked, up to a maximum of 15 extra days, or the equivalent monetary compensation.

Workers younger than 16: 20 calendar days. Workers between 16 and 18: 18 calendar days.		 Labor Code (2012).
Honduras Varying by length of tenure:
1 year: 10 working days.
2 years: 12 working days.
3 years: 15 working days.
4 or more years: 20 working days.		 Decree No. 189 (1959).
Mexico Varying by length of tenure:
- 1 year: 12 working days.
- After the first year, vacations increase by 2 days per year. Starting in the fifth year, vacations increase by 2 days per every 5 years until they reach a maximum of 32 days.		 Federal Labor Law (1970, last reformed in 2023).
Paraguay Varying by length of tenure:
- At least 1 year: 12 calendar days.
- At least 5 years: 18 calendar days.
- At least 10 years: 30 calendar days.		 Law No. 213 (2014).
Peru Workers in medium and large businesses: 30 calendar days. Workers may reduce vacation days to 15 calendar days and receive the corresponding monetary compensation for the remaining 15 days.

Workers in micro and small firms: 15 calendar days.		 Legislative Decree No. 713 (1991) - Annual leave.
Supreme Decree 013-2013-PRODUCE (2013) - Annual leave for micro and small businesses.
United States No mandatory annual leave at the Federal or State level.  
Spain 30 calendar days including public holidays. Workers’ Statute (2015, last reformed in 2024).
