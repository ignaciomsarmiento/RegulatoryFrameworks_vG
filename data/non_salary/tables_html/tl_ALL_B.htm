Chile No mandatory bonuses. At least 1 year: 15 working days. If the worker is under a fixed-term contract, 2.8% of the monthly wage is deposited into an individual severance account (this contribution is paid entirely by the employer). If the worker is under an open-term (indefinite) contract, 2.2% of the monthly wage goes to the individual severance account — 0.6% is paid by the employee and 1.6% by the employer. In addition, the employer pays a contribution to the Solidarity Unemployment Insurance Fund: 0.2% for fixed-term contracts and 0.8% for open-term contracts. Combined, total contributions equal 3.0% of wages. As of 2025, the contribution base is capped at 131.8 Unidades de Fomento (UF).

The Solidarity Fund is designed to help workers whose severance-account balances are insufficient to cover unemployment for a defined period — up to five months for workers on fixed-term contracts and up to three months for workers on open-term contracts.		 Profit Sharing: Employers must distribute 30% of the company's net taxable profits to employees annually. The distribution is proportional to each employee's remuneration. Alternatively, employers can pay a guaranteed annual bonus equivalent to 25% of each employee's annual salary. Profit sharing is capped at 4.75 MW. Law 18620 (1987) - Paid leave and profit sharing.
Law 20829 (2015) - Unemployment insurance.

Dominican Republic Christmas bonus:
- Workers earning up to 5 MW: 1 wage.
- Workers earning more than 5 MW: 5 MW.		 Varying by length of tenure:
- At least 1 year and under 5: 14 working days.
- At least 5 years: 18 working days.		 No mandatory unemployment protection. Profit sharing: Businesses must distribute 10% of their annual profits among employees. Mexico Annual bonus "Aguinaldo": 0.5 wages.

Vacation bonus: bonus equivalent to 25% of the wages corresponding to their vacation period.		 Varying by length of tenure:
- 1 year: 12 working days.
- After the first year, vacations increase by 2 days per year. Starting in the fifth year, vacations increase by 2 days per every 5 years until they reach a maximum of 32 days.		 Modality: Authorization to use retirement savings during unemployment spans of more than 45 days.

Protection granted: Workers who entered the contributory pension system at least three years before the unemployment period and have contributed for at least 12 two-month periods since are entitled to receive the equivalent of 30 daily wages with a cap at 10 Federal MW.

Workers who entered the contributory pension system at least five years before the unemployment period are entitled to claim the minimum amount between 90 daily wages (averaged over the last 250 weeks) or 11.5% of their pension savings.		 Profit sharing: Businesses must distribute 10% of their annual profits among their employees. The first 5% is distributed evenly among all eligible employees based on the number of days worked. The second 5% is distributed to employees in proportion to their annual wages. As of 2025, the total employee's payment cannot exceed the highest between three salaries and the average payment of the last three years. Peru Annual bonus: Workers in medium and large businesses: 2 wages + corresponding employer health contribution (9% or 6.75%, depending on modality), annual.

Family bonus: a monthly bonus equivalent to 10% of the MW for workers who have children under 18 or children under 24 attending tertiary education. This bonus is per worker, not per child.

Workers in small and micro businesses: 1 wage + corresponding employer health contribution (9% or 6.75%, depending on modality), annual.		 Workers in medium and large businesses: 30 calendar days. Workers may reduce vacation days to 15 calendar days and receive the corresponding monetary compensation for the remaining 15 days.

Workers in micro and small firms: 15 calendar days.		 For workers in micro businesses: None.

For workers in small businesses: Half a monthly wage goes into a severance savings account.

For workers in medium and large businesses: One monthly wage goes to a severance savings account.		 Profit sharing: Businesses with more than 20 employees must share their annual pre-tax profits with their employees as follows:
- Businesses in mining, wholesale and retail businesses, and restaurants: 8%
- Businesses in manufacturing, fishery and telecommunication: 10%
- All other businesses: 5%.
The first half is distributed among employees based on the number of days worked. The second half is distributed to employees in proportion to their annual wages.

Life insurance: All employers must pay a life insurance premium equivalent to 0.53% of a monthly wage for white-collar employees, 0.71% for blue-collar employees, and 1.46% for those performing high-risk activities. These numbers are estimations published in the Legislative Decree, but the real values that risk insurers charge may vary. Workers' dependents receive 16 monthly wages in case of natural death, or 32 monthly wages in case of accidental death or permanent disability.		 Law No. 25129 (1989) - Family Bonus.
